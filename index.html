Love to sing, dance and perform? Calisthenics is the sport for you!

Calisthenics combines lots of different styles of dance, including ballet, gymnastics and jazz. It's a team sport for beginners to advanced dancers of all ages and is perfect for anyone wanting to learn a variety of performance skills all at one convenient weekly class.

Why Sydney Calisthenics Club? No one's more passionate than us.

Our club is made up of experienced coaches and energetic young dancers. We provide a supportive environment where each performer can reach their full potential.

Want to know more? Try a class for free.

We're conveniently located in Rushcutters Bay, Sydney City. We welcome visitors and invite you to drop in on any of our classes and have a go or just watch. If you prefer, please feel free to contact us for more information.

What is calisthenics?

Calisthenics is dance which incorporates ballet, gymnastics, singing, folk dance, acting, marching, apparatus technique and performance skills. You can participate if you are three years or older. You will join a team and learn modern and fun routines choreographed to music. Our teams train once a week. The training season runs from February to October, but you can join at any time during the year.

Performance Opportunities

You can expect to dance in one competition and one concert each year. We provide beautiful costumes and you perform in theatres for supporters and, at competition time, a judge.

Benefits of Calisthenics

By dancing in a Calisthenics team, you will develop fitness, flexibility and strength and your love of artistic movement and music will increase. Your self-confidence will grow and you will make lifelong friends.

Our club

We are a creative and exciting new club led by experienced and talented coaches. We are the only club in NSW offering classes in the Sydney CBD area.

We provide a safe, supportive and fun place to develop your dance skills and meet new people. You'll experience the thrill of working together as a team, but we'll also cater for your individual abilities and celebrate your personal progress.

Whether you're new to dance or a seasoned performer, we invite you to give Calisthenics a try.

Coaches

Our coaches have over 70 years' combined dance experience with proven track records of success. Trained in Victoria, South Australia and the ACT, they bring a wealth of creativity and knowledge to Calisthenics in Sydney.

Kelly Urquhart

Kelly is excited to work with new dancers and grow Calisthenics in Sydney. She has over 30 years' experience in dance and has been coaching Calisthenics since she was 14. She spent 22 years with Canadian Bay Calisthenics College in Victoria, where she coached all age groups and soloists. When Kelly moved to Sydney in 2009, she was invited to coach at the newly formed Maverick Academy. Her senior team became State Champions in 2009 and runner-up State champs in 2012. Kelly has prepared dancers for the Australian Calisthenic Federation's practical skills exams. Achievements in calisthenics Kelly represented Victoria three times at the National Calisthenic Championships. She won "Best Club Girl" three times at Canadian Bay Calisthenics College. Kelly credits Calisthenics with helping her form many long-lasting friendships with people across Australia.

Lisa Trigg

Lisa is famed for her creative and artistic choreography and is a sought after coaching consultant. She led many teams to victory over a seven year coaching career with Aurora Calisthenics Club in Victoria, but what she really treasures is her work with dancers with serious disabilities. It's these experiences that have truly shaped her as a coach. Lisa became a qualified Aus Sport Calisthenics Coach in 2002. In 2006 she received a finalist nomination for "Most Successful Coach of the Year" by the Victorian Calisthenic Coaches Association (VCCA). In 2008 she was invited to become a member of the VCCA committee. Lisa has performed at many levels of the sport, including the elite Championship Division in Victoria at Robynmore Calisthenics College. Lisa says: "Calisthenics is more than a dance class. Not only do you get to perform on stage, but you learn what it takes to work as a team, develop commitment, form friendships and understand those around you".
